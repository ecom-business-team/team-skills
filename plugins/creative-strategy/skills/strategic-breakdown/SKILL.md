# /strategic-breakdown

Analyze a video ad creative brief or script and produce a full psychological breakdown using the Elite Video Ad Script Creator framework.

## Usage

```
/strategic-breakdown
/strategic-breakdown [paste script or describe screenshot]
```

## When to Use

- QA'ing a brief before it goes to production
- Reverse-engineering why a winning concept works
- Learning patterns from high-performing batches to replicate across clients
- Evaluating hook strength before pairing with copy

---

## Process

### Step 1: Identify the Ad's DNA

**Input needed:** Script, creative brief, or screenshot of the concept

**Do:**
Read the full concept and identify:

- **Ad Type:** General (mini-documentary, brand voice) or Organic (UGC-style, first-person discovery)
- **Format:** Voiceover or Text on Screen
- **Awareness State Targeted:** Unaware / Problem Aware / Solution Aware / Most Aware
- **Hook summary:** What is the opening doing in 3 seconds or less?
- **Hold summary:** What emotional journey does the body run?
- **CTA summary:** What action is triggered and what tension does it release?

**Output:** A 4-5 line orientation block that frames everything that follows.

---

### Step 2: Hook Analysis — The Three-Element Test

**Input needed:** The hook(s) from Step 1

**Do:**
Score and explain each hook against all three elements:

1. **Emotional Intensity** — What emotion fires immediately? (fear, curiosity, recognition, desire, shock) How strong is it? Why?
2. **Personal Relevance** — Does it call out the viewer's exact life moment? Specific or generic?
3. **Curiosity Gap** — What loop does it open? Is the gap specific enough to demand resolution without feeling manipulative?

Also note: Which Schwartz awareness state does this hook enter at? Why is that the right entry point for this audience?

**Output:** Per-hook scorecard with 2-3 sentences of explanation per element.

---

### Step 3: Emotional Journey Map

**Input needed:** The full hold/body script

**Do:**
Map the emotional arc in sequence — label each beat with the emotion it's designed to trigger and why:

```
Beat 1: [line or section] → Emotion: ___ → Why it works: ___
Beat 2: [line or section] → Emotion: ___ → Why it works: ___
...
```

Identify:
- Where does the ad **agitate pain**? (makes the problem feel urgent/physical)
- Where does it **shift the frame**? (redefines the problem or villain)
- Where does it **build desire**? (paints the transformation)
- Where does it **release tension**? (the CTA as the door they're already desperate to walk through)

**Output:** Labeled emotional arc with 5-8 beats.

---

### Step 4: Belief Ladder (Gradualization)

**Input needed:** Hold script

**Do:**
Map how the script builds belief step by step using Schwartz's gradualization principle. Identify each rung:

```
Rung 1 — Accepted truth: "___" (what the viewer already believes)
Rung 2 — Small extension: "___" (slightly larger claim, prepared by Rung 1)
Rung 3 — New frame: "___" (shifts their understanding of the problem)
Rung 4 — Mechanism: "___" (explains WHY the solution works — builds logical belief)
Rung 5 — Proof: "___" (social proof, authority, testimonial)
Rung 6 — Claim: "___" (the big payoff claim, now believable because of all prior rungs)
```

Note: Not every ad has all 6 rungs. Map what's there and flag any missing rungs that weaken the ladder.

**Output:** Labeled belief ladder with gaps flagged.

---

### Step 5: Identity Transformation

**Input needed:** Full script

**Do:**
Answer the Schwartz Identification Principle questions:

- **Current identity (pain state):** Who is the viewer being implicitly told they are right now? What identity causes them pain?
- **Desired identity (aspiration):** Who does the ad invite them to become?
- **Permission line:** What specific line(s) give them psychological permission to leave the old identity behind?
- **Purchase as self-actualization:** Does the ad frame buying as accepting who they were meant to be? Where?

**Output:** 4-part identity analysis, ~2-3 sentences each.

---

### Step 6: Fear/Desire Balance & Villain Framing

**Input needed:** Full script

**Do:**
- **Fear levers:** What specific fears are activated? (list them) Are they loss-based, identity-based, or health/safety-based?
- **Desire levers:** What specific desires are amplified? (list them) Functional → Emotional → Social → Identity
- **The villain:** Is there a villain in this story? (an ingredient, an industry, a behavior, a product category) How is it framed? Does framing the villain make the product the hero by contrast?
- **Balance assessment:** Is the ad fear-heavy, desire-heavy, or balanced? Is that the right call for this awareness state and audience?

**Output:** Bulleted fear list, desire list, villain analysis, balance verdict.

---

### Step 7: Pattern Interrupts & Curiosity Gaps

**Input needed:** Hook + Hold

**Do:**
Identify every deliberate pattern interrupt and information gap used:

- **Pattern interrupts:** Moments that break expected ad structure — a provocative question, a contradiction, a confession, an unexpected character/voice
- **Curiosity gaps:** Information loops that are opened and strategically closed (or left open to pull viewers forward)
- **Zeigarnik triggers:** Incomplete information that creates mental discomfort until resolved

For each one, note: where it appears, what it does psychologically, and whether it's executed well or could be sharper.

**Output:** Annotated list of interrupts and gaps.

---

### Step 8: Unconscious Associations & Deep-Seated Beliefs

**Input needed:** Full script

**Do:**
Surface what's operating below the surface:

- **Unconscious associations triggered:** What does the product/solution get mentally linked to? (authority, nature, science, community, identity groups, historical proof) List the associations and the specific lines that trigger them.
- **Deep-seated beliefs leveraged:** What does the viewer already believe (about themselves, the world, the problem category) that this script is using as leverage? List each belief and the line that activates it.
- **Tribal/identity signals:** Does the script use group membership language? ("dog owners like you," "men who take this seriously") What tribe does it invite them to join?

**Output:** Two lists (associations + beliefs) plus tribal signal note.

---

### Step 9: Overall Verdict & Replication Insights

**Input needed:** All prior steps

**Do:**
Deliver the synthesis:

- **What makes this concept strong** (2-3 specific reasons, tied to the framework)
- **What could be sharper** (any weak rungs, thin belief ladders, gaps that don't close)
- **Replication pattern** — What is the core formula of this concept that could be adapted for another client or product? State it as a transferable template: _"[Villain/Problem frame] → [Mechanism reveal] → [Product as evolved solution]"_
- **Awareness state fit** — Is this the right approach for where this audience is? Any risk of misalignment?

**Output:** 4-part verdict section.

---

## Quality Check

Before delivering:
- [ ] Every hook is scored against all three elements (intensity, relevance, gap)
- [ ] The emotional journey has at least 5 labeled beats
- [ ] The belief ladder has rungs identified AND gaps flagged
- [ ] The identity section has a specific "permission line" quoted from the script
- [ ] The replication insight is stated as a transferable formula, not just a compliment
- [ ] The verdict includes at least one thing that could be sharper (no brief is perfect)

---

## Notes

- This skill is used across 6 clients: 3 pet brands + 3 Keeps telehealth brands. Schwartz principles apply universally but the awareness state, villain framing, and tribal language will differ by category.
- For Keeps specifically: audience is solution-aware to most-aware men with hair loss; the villain is usually DHT, finasteride limitations, or systemic hair loss category messaging
- For pet brands: audience is often unaware/problem-aware; emotional investment in the pet is the primary lever
- When analyzing a screenshot (rather than full text), work with what's visible and note any sections where copy is truncated or unclear
- The goal is always: what is the replicable formula? Every breakdown should end with something actionable
